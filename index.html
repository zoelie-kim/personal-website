Hi! I'm Zoelie, a Dean's List student at UC Berkeley studying Data Science and Philosophy with an interest in shaping technology that centers human wellbeing and ensuring it's deployed responsibly.

Since I can remember, I've always had an instinct to learn. In first grade, I begged my mom to let me watch YouTube videos on how to complete a special knitting stitch because they didn't teach it until the next grade and I couldn't wait. My love of learning has only deepened over time. I get energized tracking variables in a programming environment diagram and grappling with philosophical proof reconstruction. This has led me to become an interdisciplinary learner at my core and has led me to teaching.

As a teaching assistant, I learned what resonates with students. I picked up which final few words make a concept click or how to sketch a visual that intuitively explains an idea. Through my data science coursework and ML projects, I've learned how to bring this skill to different domains. In my club, I help technical builders understand the human impact and ethical implications of what they create by connecting their technical decisions to real human consequences and AI alignment concerns. In my go-to-market work, I enjoy translating technical capabilities into language that helps audiences understand the value in both the product and the vision.

I'm drawn to working between different worlds because that's where meaningful solutions exist and where I feel most engaged. As I continue building, I'm collecting these experiences intentionally so I can develop the discernment and expertise that comes from understanding the full picture.
